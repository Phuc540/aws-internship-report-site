# Summary Report: “Amazon Web Services (AWS): Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Applications”

### Event Objectives

- Help students better understand the cloud market, career trends, and recruitment demand in the technology field.
- Provide practical perspectives on enterprise cloud architecture and how cloud is applied across different industries.
- Share the differences between academic knowledge at school and real working requirements in enterprises.
- Introduce real-world experience in building Data Platforms in business environments through sharing from Renova Cloud.
- Help students reduce the pressure of comparing themselves with great role models and focus on developing their own strengths.
- Share the skills, mindset, and roadmap students need to prepare better for the AI era.

### Speakers

- **Nguyen Gia Hung** – Head of Solutions Architect in Vietnam & Cambodia, Amazon Web Services
- **Vinh Banh** – Senior Data Engineer, Renova Cloud
- **Nhu Tran** – Account Manager, Amazon Web Services
- **Khang Nguyen** – Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics

### Key Highlights

#### Job Market & Career Trends

The sharing session by **Nguyen Gia Hung** focused on the job market and career trends in cloud, AI, and information technology. The speaker presented the growth of the cloud market in Vietnam, recruitment demand in the industry, in-demand roles, and the skill roadmap that students should prepare.

Key points included:

- The cloud market in Vietnam is growing quickly and creating many new job opportunities.
- AWS has had a long-term investment and development journey in the region, contributing to the growth of the cloud ecosystem.
- Many businesses in fields such as technology, finance, e-commerce, manufacturing, and startups are using cloud for digital transformation.
- Roles such as Cloud Engineer, Solutions Architect, DevOps Engineer, Data Engineer, Software Engineer, and Security Engineer are in high demand.
- Students need to prepare technical foundations, communication skills, continuous learning mindset, and adaptability to market changes.
- AWS certifications can help students demonstrate their capabilities and gain an advantage when applying for jobs.

Through this session, I understood more clearly that cloud is not only a new technology, but also an important foundation in the operations of modern businesses. To take advantage of opportunities in this field, students need to learn with a clear direction, combining fundamental knowledge, hands-on labs, certifications, and real projects.

#### School vs Industry – Same Skills, Different Game

The sharing session by **Vinh Banh** helped me better understand the differences between the learning environment at school and the working environment in enterprises. Although many fundamental skills are learned at university, applying them in real work is more complex and requires additional skills.

Key points included:

- At school, students often work with assignments, sample data, and clearly described requirements.
- In enterprises, requirements may change frequently, and data is often not as clean or complete as in school assignments.
- Fundamental skills such as databases, programming, APIs, systems, and logical thinking are still very important.
- When working in a real business environment, students need to learn how to handle practical requirements, complex data, and communication with different stakeholders.
- Knowing how to use tools is not enough; what matters more is understanding how to build a complete system.
- An engineer needs the ability to analyze problems, understand the business context, and propose suitable solutions.

This session helped me realize that knowledge learned at school is a very necessary foundation. However, to work effectively in enterprises, students need to know how to apply that knowledge to real situations, communicate clearly, and adapt to changes during the working process.

#### Real-world Data Platform Through My Journey

In the next sharing session, **Vinh Banh** also presented his journey of working with real-world data platforms in different environments. This content helped me visualize how a data system is designed, implemented, and operated in enterprises.

Key points included:

- A real-world Data Platform does not only include databases, but also many components such as data ingestion, data processing, storage, governance, analytics, and monitoring.
- In a startup environment, engineers often need to participate in many parts of the system and handle work flexibly.
- In large enterprises, systems need to focus on stability, security, scalability, and data governance.
- In consulting environments, engineers need to understand customer problems, propose suitable architectures, and implement solutions based on real needs.
- A Data Platform must be built based on both technical factors and business goals.
- Building a data system requires system thinking, analytical ability, and collaboration with many teams.

Through this session, I understood that a Data Platform is a large system with many processing layers. To perform well in this field, an engineer not only needs to know tools, but also needs to understand data, operations, and the value that the system brings to the business.

#### How Can We Have Vietnamese Steve Jobs?

The sharing session by **Nhu Tran** brought a different perspective on personal development and career orientation. Instead of encouraging students to become a “Vietnamese Steve Jobs” or a version of famous figures such as Steve Jobs or Elon Musk, she emphasized that each person should not use someone else’s name as a standard to compare themselves with.

Key points included:

- Students should not put themselves on a scale against great role models such as Steve Jobs or Elon Musk.
- Comparing oneself with others can create unnecessary pressure.
- Each individual should focus on understanding their own abilities, interests, strengths, and personal values.
- Instead of trying to become another person’s version, students should try to become the best version of themselves.
- Each person can grow on their own path, suitable for their personality, abilities, and personal goals.
- The sharing session helped students reduce peer pressure and remove mindset barriers in the process of future orientation.

Through this session, I realized that having a role model to learn from is good, but we should not turn that role model into pressure or a required standard for ourselves. What matters more is understanding who we are, what our strengths are, and what direction we want to grow in. Instead of trying to become a version of someone else, each student should try to become the best version of themselves.

#### AI-Ready Freshers – Skills and Mindset in the AI Era

The sharing session by **Khang Nguyen** focused on the skills and mindset that fresh graduates need in the AI era. The speaker emphasized that AI is changing the way people work, but what matters is that students need to adapt, learn, and develop their own capabilities.

Key points included:

- Students need to prepare both technical knowledge and a professional working attitude.
- In the AI era, learners need a continuous learning mindset and the ability to adapt quickly.
- AI is a powerful supporting tool, but it should not be seen as a complete replacement for human thinking.
- Students need to know how to use AI to increase productivity, learn faster, and improve work quality.
- Companies evaluate candidates not only through knowledge, but also through attitude, responsibility, learning ability, and problem-solving skills.
- A good fresher needs a growth mindset, communication ability, responsibility, and proactiveness at work.

This session helped me understand that in the AI era, students should not only worry about being replaced by AI. Instead, the important thing is to learn how to use AI as a supporting tool while continuing to develop professional foundations, soft skills, and problem-solving mindset.

### Key Takeaways

#### Cloud Market and Career Opportunities

- Cloud is becoming an important field in Vietnam’s digital transformation process.
- AWS and cloud platforms create many career opportunities for information technology students.
- Roles such as Cloud Engineer, Solutions Architect, DevOps Engineer, Data Engineer, and Software Engineer are all in high demand.
- Students need to prepare technical skills, certifications, real projects, and communication ability.
- Joining technology communities helps students expand their knowledge and career opportunities.

#### Practical Skills in Enterprises

- Knowledge at school is the foundation, but business environments require more flexible application.
- Data, requirements, and processes in enterprises are often more complex than school assignments.
- Engineers need to know how to work with changing requirements, incomplete data, and deadline pressure.
- Communication and coordination with different stakeholders are very important skills.
- Knowing tools is not enough; understanding how to build a complete system is more important.

#### Personal Development Mindset

- Students should not compare themselves too much with famous role models or other people’s success.
- Each person has their own strengths, goals, and development path.
- Peer pressure can make students lose direction if they do not understand themselves well.
- Instead of trying to become a copy of someone else, students should focus on becoming the best version of themselves.
- Understanding oneself is an important step in building a suitable career roadmap.

#### Mindset in the AI Era

- AI is a powerful supporting tool, but it does not completely replace human thinking.
- Students need to learn how to use AI to increase productivity and improve work quality.
- In the AI era, learners need to continuously update their knowledge and adapt to change.
- Employers still highly value attitude, problem-solving mindset, responsibility, and learning ability.
- A good candidate needs to combine technical skills, soft skills, and a growth mindset.

### Development Workflow

- When planning a career, students should start by understanding the market, job roles, and real recruitment requirements.
- Learning cloud should follow a roadmap from foundation to advanced knowledge, combining theory, hands-on labs, certifications, and personal projects.
- When building systems in enterprises, it is necessary to understand requirements, data, users, business goals, and system scalability.
- For a Data Platform, the workflow usually includes data ingestion, data processing, storage, governance, analytics, visualization, and monitoring.
- In personal development, students need to understand their strengths, weaknesses, interests, and goals.
- In the AI era, learners need to know how to use AI as a supporting tool while still maintaining critical thinking and self-learning ability.
