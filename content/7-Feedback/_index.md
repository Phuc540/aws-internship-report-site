In this section, I would like to share my personal feedback and experience after participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program. This internship was a valuable opportunity for me to learn, practice, and grow in a professional environment. Through the program, I gained not only technical knowledge but also practical working experience, teamwork skills, and a clearer career direction in the technology field.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  

The working environment at FCAJ was friendly, open, and supportive. During the internship, I felt comfortable asking questions whenever I faced difficulties. The mentors, admins, and other members were willing to support interns during the learning and project development process.

The program also created a good environment for students to experience a real working atmosphere. I had the chance to work on project tasks, update my worklog, participate in sharing sessions, and learn from people with practical experience in the IT industry.

However, because there were many interns joining the program, I felt that there were still some limitations in the number of events or Community Day sessions that every intern could fully participate in. Personally, I found these sessions very useful because they provided knowledge that was not limited to technical topics, but also included career orientation, communication skills, teamwork, and real working experience from professionals.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  

The support from mentors and the admin team was one of the most valuable parts of the internship. My mentor provided guidance when I had questions about tasks, project direction, and technical problems. Instead of only giving direct answers, the mentor encouraged me to think, explore solutions, and solve problems by myself. This helped me become more independent and confident when handling assigned tasks.

The admin team also supported interns well with announcements, documentation, event organization, and internship-related procedures. Their support helped the internship process become clearer and more organized.

I especially appreciate the patience, responsibility, and enthusiasm of the mentors and admins throughout the program. Their feedback helped me improve my working attitude, task handling, and project contribution.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  

The tasks assigned to me were closely related to my academic major and career direction. In the GreenLens Kids project, I worked mainly as a Frontend Developer, participating in UI design, UI/UX improvement, and API integration for user-facing features.

The project tasks helped me apply what I had learned at university into a real product development process. I had the chance to work with Figma, build frontend interfaces, validate user input, handle loading and error states, integrate APIs, and test feature flows.

In addition, the internship also exposed me to new knowledge about AWS Cloud, DevOps, AI, cloud architecture, and modern development workflows. These are areas that are useful for my future career in software development.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  

During the internship, I learned many new skills and improved several existing ones. Technically, I improved my frontend development skills, UI/UX thinking, API integration ability, and understanding of how frontend features connect with backend services.

Through AWS Skill Builder labs, I gained more knowledge about cloud computing, AWS services, security, AI services, monitoring, and architecture concepts. These learning activities helped me better understand how cloud services can support real-world applications.

Besides technical skills, I also developed soft skills such as teamwork, communication, time management, problem-solving, and reporting progress. The sharing sessions and events also helped me gain more perspectives about career preparation, DevOps, data analytics, and working culture in multinational companies.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  

The culture during the internship was positive and encouraging. Everyone respected each other and was willing to share knowledge. Even though interns came from different backgrounds and skill levels, the program created a collaborative environment where everyone could learn and improve together.

In the project team, I learned the importance of communication and coordination. When working on frontend tasks, I needed to discuss requirements, understand API flows, report bugs, and coordinate with other members to complete features. This helped me understand that software development is not only about writing code, but also about teamwork and clear communication.

The team spirit in the program motivated me to be more responsible with my tasks and more proactive in learning.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  

The internship program provided many useful learning opportunities. The combination of project work, AWS Skill Builder labs, mentor support, events, and community activities made the internship experience more meaningful.

I also appreciated the opportunity to participate in technical sharing sessions and Community Day activities. These sessions helped interns learn from experienced speakers and understand more about real working environments.

Overall, the program provided a good balance between learning, practicing, and career development.

### Additional Questions

**What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  

The most satisfying part of my internship was the opportunity to work on the GreenLens Kids project and contribute to real frontend features. I was able to participate in designing and improving user interfaces, integrating APIs, and testing feature flows.

I also appreciated the learning environment of the program. The mentors, admins, and other interns created a supportive atmosphere that encouraged me to ask questions, learn from mistakes, and keep improving. The events and sharing sessions were also very valuable because they helped me understand more about the IT industry and future career opportunities.

**What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  

I think the program could organize more sharing sessions, Community Day activities, or technical workshops for interns. These events are very helpful because they allow interns to learn from experienced professionals, ask questions, and gain knowledge beyond their assigned project tasks.

In addition, it would be useful if there were more sessions focused on practical project workflows, such as how to work with Git effectively, how to write better documentation, how to communicate technical issues, and how to prepare a portfolio from internship projects.

The schedule for events could also be more flexible, so that more interns can participate without being limited by weekend availability.

**If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?**  

Yes, I would recommend this internship program to my friends. The program provides a good environment for students to learn, practice, and experience real project work. Interns can receive support from mentors, participate in useful events, and improve both technical and soft skills.

I think this program is especially suitable for students who want to learn more about AWS Cloud, software development, teamwork, and career preparation. It is also a good opportunity to understand how technology is applied in real projects and how to develop a more professional working mindset.

### Suggestions & Expectations

**Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  

Yes. If I have the opportunity, I would be happy to continue participating in this program in the future. This internship has been a valuable experience that helped me grow both technically and professionally.

Through the program, I became more confident in frontend development, API integration, UI/UX improvement, and teamwork. I also gained a clearer understanding of AWS Cloud, DevOps, AI, and career development in the IT industry.

In the future, I hope the program will continue to provide more opportunities for interns to join real projects, attend technical workshops, participate in community events, and receive guidance from experienced mentors.
